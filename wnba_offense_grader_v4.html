Shooter-Adjusted Expected (SAXE) — Takes every shot location the team creates and asks: what's the expected value given the actual player shooting it? Uses real per-player zone shooting splits from leaguedashplayershotlocations — Left Corner 3, Right Corner 3, and Above the Break 3 are tracked separately, so handedness and directional tendencies are real, not estimated. The Δ column shows whether a team's shooters are lifting or wasting the system's looks.

Data pipeline

On load, the app fires two parallel API calls to stats.wnba.com via CORS proxies:

1. leaguedashteamshotlocations — team-level FGM/FGA/FG% for 7 zones (Restricted Area, Paint non-RA, Mid-Range, Left Corner 3, Right Corner 3, Above the Break 3, Backcourt). This feeds SQE.

2. leaguedashplayershotlocations — same 7 zones but per player. Every player's zone shooting % is mapped to their team. The team SAXE is a usage-weighted average of each player's expected output in the system's shot distribution. This feeds SAXE.

A third call fetches advanced team stats (offensive rating, pace, TOV%, AST%) from leaguedashteamstats.

1.14
Corner 3s have the shortest distance and highest make rate among threes (~38%). Tracked separately for handedness.
Right Corner 3
1.14
Same as left corner but right side. In SAXE, a player's left vs. right corner rate is used from their actual shot chart.
Above the Break 3
1.06
All non-corner 3s — wings, top of key. Slightly lower make rate than corners. Highest volume of 3-point attempts.
SQE formula: For each team, Σ(zone FGA share × zone xPPS × point value), normalized so league avg = 100.

SAXE formula: Same shot distribution as SQE, but zone xPPS is replaced by each shooter's actual FG% in that zone × point value, weighted by their share of team FGA in that zone. Career/3-yr mode blends prior seasons (60% recent, 40% prior) to smooth small-sample noise.

Contest data gap: Closest-defender distance per shot comes from Second Spectrum's proprietary player tracking system. It's available to teams and licensed researchers but not via any public API. ShotQuality.com has built a computer-vision version for WNBA (paid). Until that data is accessible, SQE treats all shots in a zone equally regardless of contest level — which is the same limitation all public basketball analytics tools share.

Garbage time: When toggled off, applies a team-specific possession adjustment based on margin-of-victory profile (teams that win/lose big run different offenses late). Not play-level filtering since that requires full PbP access.

TOV Damage: (Weighted TOV/g × 0.6) + (Opp pts off TOV/g × 0.4), normalized to 100. Live-ball TOVs estimated from opponent steal data and weighted 1.4×.
